Inland Taipan

Most venomous snake in the world; extremely fast and agile.

  • Scientific name: Oxyuranus microlepidotus
  • Average size: c. 1.8 metres (5.9 ft), larger specimens up to 2.5 metres (8.2 ft)
  • Color: dark tan, ranging from a rich, dark hue to a brownish light-green, depending on season
  • Habitat: Australia (semi-arid regions where Queensland and South Australia borders converge)

The inland taipan is the most venomous snake in the world; it is an extremely fast and agile snake that can strike instantly with extreme accuracy, often striking multiple times in the same attack, and it envenoms in almost every case. Although extremely venomous and a capable striker, in contrast to the rather aggressive coastal taipan, the inland taipan is usually quite a shy and reclusive snake, with a placid disposition, and prefers to escape from trouble.
